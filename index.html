I am an assistant professor at the Yau Mathematical Sciences Center at Tsinghua University in Beijing since December 2021. I was previously at UC Berkeley as a Morrey visiting assistant professor, and did my PhD with Professor Shing-Tung Yau at Harvard.

My research interest lies broadly in algebraic geometry, dynamical systems, symplectic geometry, and mathematical physics. I am particularly interested in derived categories, Bridgeland stability conditions, mirror symmetry, and categorical dynamical systems.

Publications and Preprints

  1. Systolic inequalities for K3 surfaces via stability conditions.
      Math. Z., 300 (2022), 301-323. [+]
      We introduce the notions of categorical systoles and categorical volumes of Bridgeland stability conditions on triangulated categories. We prove a systolic inequality for any complex projective K3 surface, which is an algebro-geometric generalization of Loewner's torus systolic inequality. We also discuss applications of this inequality in symplectic geometry.

  2. On pseudo-Anosov autoequivalences (with Simion Filip, Fabian Haiden, Ludmil Katzarkov, and Yijia Liu).
      Adv. Math., 384 (2021), 107732. [+]
      Motivated by results of Thurston, we prove that any autoequivalence of a triangulated category induces a filtration by triangulated subcategories, provided the existence of Bridgeland stability conditions. The filtration is given by the exponential growth rate of masses under iterates of the autoequivalence, and only depends on the choice of a connected component of the stability manifold. We then propose a new definition of pseudo-Anosov autoequivalences, and prove that our definition is more general than the one previously proposed by Dimitrov, Haiden, Katzarkov, and Kontsevich. We construct new examples of pseudo-Anosov autoequivalences on the derived categories of quintic Calabi-Yau threefolds and quiver Calabi-Yau categories. Finally, we prove that certain pseudo-Anosov autoequivalences on quiver 3-Calabi-Yau categories act hyperbolically on the space of Bridgeland stability conditions.

  3. Categorical polynomial entropy (with Lie Fu and Genki Ouchi).
      Adv. Math., 383 (2021), 107655. [+]
      For classical dynamical systems, the polynomial entropy serves as a refined invariant of the topological entropy. In the setting of categorical dynamical systems, that is, triangulated categories endowed with an endofunctor, we develop the theory of categorical polynomial entropy, refining the categorical entropy defined by Dimitrov-Haiden-Katzarkov-Kontsevich. We justify this notion by showing that for an automorphism of a smooth projective variety, the categorical polynomial entropy of the pullback functor on the derived category coincides with the polynomial growth rate of the induced action on cohomology. We also establish in general a Yomdin-type lower bound for the categorical polynomial entropy of an endofunctor in terms of the induced endomorphism on the numerical Grothendieck group of the category. As examples, we compute the categorical polynomial entropy for some standard functors like shifts, Serre functors, tensoring line bundles, automorphisms, spherical twists, P-twists, and so on, illustrating clearly how categorical polynomial entropy refines the study of categorical entropy and enables us to study the phenomenon of categorical trichotomy. A parallel theory of polynomial mass growth rate is developed in the presence of Bridgeland stability conditions.

  4. Weil-Petersson geometry on the space of Bridgeland stability conditions (with Atsushi Kanazawa and Shing-Tung Yau).
      Comm. Anal. Geom., 29 (2021), no. 3, 681-706. [+]
      Inspired by mirror symmetry, we investigate some differential geometric aspects of the space of Bridgeland stability conditions on a Calabi-Yau triangulated category. The aim is to develop theory of Weil-Petersson geometry on the stringy Kähler moduli space. A few basic examples are studied. In particular, we identify our Weil-Petersson metric with the Bergman metric on a Siegel modular variety in the case of the self-product of an elliptic curve.

  5. Mirror of Atiyah flop in symplectic geometry and stability conditions (with Hansol Hong, Siu-Cheong Lau, and Shing-Tung Yau).
      Adv. Theor. Math. Phys., 22 (2018), no. 5, 1149-1207. [+]
      We study the mirror operation of the Atiyah flop in symplectic geometry. We formulate the operation for a symplectic manifold with a Lagrangian fibration. Furthermore we construct geometric stability conditions on the derived Fukaya category of the deformed conifold and study the action of the mirror Atiyah flop on these stability conditions.

  6. Entropy of an autoequivalence on Calabi-Yau manifolds.
      Math. Res. Lett., 25 (2018), no. 2, 509-519. [+]
      We construct the first counterexamples of a conjecture on categorical entropy by Kikuta and Takahashi. The result shows that because of the existence of `hidden symmetries' on Calabi-Yau manifolds (autoequivalences that are not generated by tensoring line bundles, automorphisms, and shiftings), the autoequivalences on the derived categories contain much richer dynamical information than the automorphisms on Calabi-Yau manifolds.

  7. An invitation to categorical dynamical systems. In preparation.
      Proceedings of the 2021 Ergodic Theory and Dynamical Systems workshop. [+]
      The goal of this article is to invite the reader to get to know and get involved into categorical dynamical systems by describing some of its recent development and open problems.

  9. Counting special Lagrangian classes and semistable Mukai vectors for K3 surfaces (with Jayadev S. Athreya and Heather Lee).
      Submitted. [+]
      Motivated by the study of growth rate of the number of geodesics in flat surfaces with bounded lengths, we study generalizations of such problems for K3 surfaces. In one generalization, we give an upper bound on the asymptotics of the number of irreducible special Lagrangian classes in K3 surfaces with bounded period integrals. In another generalization, we give the leading term of the counting function of the number of semistable classes with bounded central charges for generic Bridgeland stability conditions on algebraic K3 surfaces.

  10. Attractor mechanisms of moduli spaces of Calabi-Yau 3-folds (with Atsushi Kanazawa).
      Submitted. [+]
      We investigate the complex and Kähler attractor mechanisms of moduli spaces of Calabi-Yau 3-folds. The complex attractor mechanism was previously studied by Ferrara-Kallosh-Strominger, Moore and others in string theory. It is concerned with the minimizing problems of the normalized central charges of 3-cycles and defines a new interesting class of Calabi-Yau 3-folds called, the complex attractor varieties. In light of mirror symmetry, we introduce the Kähler attractor mechanism and define the Kähler attractor varieties. The complex and Kähler attractor varieties are expected to possess very rich structures, in particular certain complex and Kähler rigidities.

  11. Surfaces, braids, Stokes matrices, and points on spheres (with Junho Peter Whang).
      Submitted. [+]
      Moduli spaces of points on n-spheres carry natural actions of braid groups. For n=0, 1, and 3, we prove that these symmetries extend to actions of mapping class groups of positive genus surfaces, through isomorphisms with certain moduli of local systems. This relies on the existence of group structure for spheres in these dimensions. We also use the connection to demonstrate that the space of rank 4 Stokes matrices with fixed Coxeter invariant of nonzero discriminant contains only finitely many integral braid group orbits.

  12. Asymptotic shifting numbers in triangulated categories (with Simion Filip).
      Submitted. [+]
      We introduce invariants, called shifting numbers, that measure the asymptotic amount by which an autoequivalence of a triangulated category translates inside the category. The invariants are analogous to Poincare translation numbers that are widely used in dynamical systems. We additionally establish that in some examples the shifting numbers provide a quasimorphism on the group of autoequivalences. Additionally, we relate our shifting numbers to the entropy function introduced by Dimitrov, Haiden, Katzarkov, and Kontsevich.

  13. New rational cubic fourfolds arising from Cremona transformations (with Kuan-Wen Lai).
      Submitted. [+]
      Are Fourier-Mukai equivalent cubic fourfolds birationally equivalent? We obtain an affirmative answer to this question for very general cubic fourfolds of discriminant 20, where we produce birational maps via the Cremona transformation defined by the Veronese surface. Moreover, by studying how these maps act on the cubics known to be rational, we found new rational examples.

  14. Contractibility of space of stability conditions on the projective plane via global dimension function (with Chunyi Li, Wanmin Liu, and Yu Qiu).
      Submitted. [+]
      We give an alternate proof of the contractibility of the principal component of the space of Bridgeland stability conditions on the derived category of projective plane by examine the global dimension function introduced by Ikeda-Qiu. Furthermore, we show that most of the stability conditions on local projective plane can be induced from stability conditions on projective plane by Ikeda-Qiu's inducing theorem.

  15. On entropy of P-twists.
      Preprint. [+]
      P-twists are the mirror of Dehn twists along Lagrangian complex projective space under mirror symmetry. We compute the categorical entropy of autoequivalences given by P-twists, and show that these autoequivalences satisfy a Gromov-Yomdin type conjecture.
