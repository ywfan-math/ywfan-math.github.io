I am an assistant professor at Yau Mathematical Sciences Center of Tsinghua University in Beijing. I was previously at UC Berkeley as a Morrey Visiting Assistant Professor, and did my PhD with Professor Shing-Tung Yau at Harvard.

My research interest lies broadly in algebraic geometry, dynamical systems, symplectic geometry, and mathematical physics. I am particularly interested in derived categories, Bridgeland stability conditions, mirror symmetry, and categorical dynamical systems.

Publications and Preprints

  • Compact moduli of K3 surfaces with a nonsymplectic automorphism (with Valery Alexeev and Changho Han).
      Submitted, 2021. [+]
      We study moduli spaces of K3 surfaces with a non-symplectic automorphism fixing a curve of genus at least two. We show that such a moduli space has a canonically defined compactification by KSBA stable pairs. Furthermore, we prove that this compactification is a Looijenga semitoroidal compactification for some semifan.
