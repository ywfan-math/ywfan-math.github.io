I am a postdoc at the University of Bonn as part of the ERC Synergy grant HyperK. Previously, I was a visiting assistant professor at UMass Amherst. I obtained my PhD in 2018 from Brown University under the supervision of Brendan Hassett.

I am an algebraic geometer in the broadest sense. Since I was a PhD student, I am interested in topics relevant to cubic fourfolds and K3 surfaces, with a special focus on the symmetries of their moduli spaces induced by lattice isometries, derived equivalences, and birational maps. During my stay at UMass Amherst, I started to expend the scope of my research to algebraic geometry over nonclosed fields. Currently, I am studying (1) classifications of autoequivalences via their actions on Bridgeland stability conditions, (2) birational geometry of moduli of hyperkähler manifolds, especially the irrationality, and (3) birational permutations, namely, birational automorphisms of an algebraic variety which induce bijections on rational points.

I am originally from Taiwan. More about me can be found in my curriculum vitae.
