Online Seminar on Algebraic and Complex Dynamics

Description

Welcome to the Online Seminar on Algebraic and Complex Dynamics.

This is a new seminar, centered loosely around algebraic dynamics, complex dynamics, and related topics. We meet weekly for a 90-minute talk, with the first 30 minutes aiming towards non-experts and graduate students. After the talk we will have 30 minutes of informal discussion.

The seminar usually takes place on Tuesdays, either at 3pm in Beijing/Taipei (= UTC 7am) or at 9am in Beijing/Taipei (= UTC 1am), depending on where the speakers are located. The talks will be hosted on Zoom. All are welcome to tune in.

Upcoming Talk

October 25 at 9am in Beijing/Taipei

Salim Tayou (Harvard University)

Title: Equidistribution of Hodge loci

Abstract: Given a polarized variation of Hodge structures, the Hodge locus is a countable union of proper algebraic subvarieties where extra Hodge classes appear. In this talk, I will explain a general equidistribution theorem for these Hodge loci and explain several applications: equidistribution of higher codimension Noether-Lefschetz loci, equidistribution of Hecke translates of a curve in the moduli space of abelian varieties and equidistribution of some families of CM points in Shimura varieties. The results of this talk are joint work with Nicolas Tholozan.
